Syria strike delayed; Obama evaluates proposal for surrender of chemical weapons

Updates

  • Syria accepts a proposal by Russia to surrender it's chemical weapons to avert military action

Summary of events

Syria erupted in protest as part of the arab spring in March 2011, but protestors were met with lethal force and the country erupted into civil war. In July rebels formed the Free Syrian Army to fight the government.

In the months that followed, reports of chemical weapons attacks began to surface. August 20th 2012, Obama warned that the use of chemical weapons by the Syrian government would be met with "enormous consequences".

August 21st 2013 reports emerged that hundreds (the exact figure is disputed) had died in what appeared to be a chemical weapons attack. President Obama began looking for approval from congress and their constituents for a military strike on Syria. A resolution to authorize military force against the Syrian government was introduced to the Senate.

On Tuesday September 10th Obama addressed the nation. Amidst low approval ratings for a military strike on Syria he said "This would be a targeted strike to achieve a clear objective: deterring the use of chemical weapons and degrading Assad's capabilities,".

Latest update

Russia has proposed Syria surrender it's chemical weapons to avert military action. Obama has asked for a delay on the vote to authorize a strike on Syria while he determines the credibility of the proposal. He said “It’s too early to tell whether this offer will succeed, and any agreement must verify that the Assad regime keeps its commitments”.
